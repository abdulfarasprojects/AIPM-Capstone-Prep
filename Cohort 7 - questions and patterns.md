## Main patterns across the demo questions

### 1. **Is this a real pain, for a real user?**
Judges kept testing:
- who exactly the user is
- how painful the problem is
- whether demand was validated with real users

Examples:
- “Who is the ICP?”
- “How was demand determined?”
- “What size practices are you targeting?”

### 2. **Why this product vs a generic AI tool?**
They pushed hard on differentiation:
- why this is vertical-specific
- why incumbents or horizontal tools can’t easily copy it
- what the moat is

Examples:
- “What makes this unique for that vertical?”
- “How does this build a moat?”
- “Why isn’t this just a horizontal voice system?”

### 3. **What is the wedge / sharpest entry point?**
They wanted focus:
- what feature gets adoption first
- what the initial wedge is
- whether the team is doing too much at once

Examples:
- “If you had to pick one wedge feature…?”
- “Why wasn’t that the primary focus?”
- questions about ambitious scope vs focused scope

### 4. **How does it actually fit into the workflow?**
A big pattern was workflow realism:
- where this fits in the user journey
- onboarding friction
- whether AI feels native or bolted on
- what happens after the model output

Examples:
- “How are users onboarded?”
- “What actions happen after the agent identifies issues?”
- “Have you seen friction from asking users to adopt a broader workflow?”

### 5. **How do you know it works?**
They repeatedly probed evaluation quality:
- what metrics are used
- whether testing goes beyond happy paths
- whether real-world outcomes are measured

Examples:
- “How will you evaluate real performance?”
- “How did you choose the testing scenarios?”
- “Have you done adversarial testing?”

### 6. **How do you handle failure, risk, and edge cases?**
Especially for voice, healthcare, kids, and compliance:
- safety
- jailbreaks
- inappropriate outputs
- escalation to humans
- low-confidence handling

Examples:
- “Where do you defer to humans?”
- “How do you ensure safety?”
- “How do you protect PII?”
- “How do you know data is good enough?”

### 7. **What are the economics and commercialization path?**
They looked for business clarity:
- monetization logic
- go-to-market path
- pricing rationale
- ROI for customers

Examples:
- “What does a typical story cost?”
- “How do you monetize extraction vs intelligence?”
- “How are you marketing this product?”

### 8. **Is the demo polished, clear, and credible?**
Questions often exposed presentation quality too:
- whether the demo actually showed the differentiated value
- whether the story was clear
- whether the product looked end-to-end and believable

Examples:
- feedback that a demo felt too generic
- requests to clarify architecture, flow, or product boundaries

## Overall takeaway
The judges were mostly stress-testing **8 things**:

1. **User**
2. **Pain**
3. **Differentiation**
4. **Focus**
5. **Workflow fit**
6. **Evaluation**
7. **Risk handling**
8. **Business viability**



## Judge-Question Cheat Sheet by Theme

### 1. ICP / User
- Who exactly is the user?
- Who is the buyer?
- What segment are you targeting first?
- What size company/practice/customer are you focused on?
- Is there a narrower ICP inside the broader category?
- Who feels this pain most acutely?

### 2. Problem / Pain
- What is the core pain point?
- How painful is this problem really?
- Is this a painkiller or a vitamin?
- What happens today if this problem is not solved?
- How frequently does this problem occur?
- Why is this urgent now?

### 3. Validation / Discovery
- How did you determine demand for this?
- How many users/customers did you talk to?
- What did users consistently say?
- What evidence made you confident this is the right problem?
- What surprised you in discovery?
- What did you learn that changed the product direction?

### 4. Differentiation / Moat
- Why is this better than a generic AI tool?
- What makes this vertical-specific?
- Why can’t an incumbent or larger platform replicate this?
- What is your moat?
- What proprietary data, workflow, or distribution advantage do you have?
- Why will users choose this over existing alternatives?

### 5. Wedge / Product Focus
- What is the wedge feature?
- If you had to launch with just one feature, what would it be?
- What is the initial entry point into the market?
- Are you trying to do too much too early?
- What would you cut for an MVP?
- Why is this the right place to start?

### 6. Workflow / UX Fit
- Where does this fit in the user’s workflow?
- How do users get onboarded?
- Does this reduce work or add another step?
- What happens after the model produces an output?
- How do users take action on the output?
- Is the AI integrated naturally into the workflow or bolted on?

### 7. Output Quality / Evaluation
- How are you evaluating output quality?
- What metrics matter most?
- Are you measuring real outcomes or just usage?
- How did you choose your eval scenarios?
- Are you testing beyond the happy path?
- What does success look like in production?

### 8. Safety / Reliability / Edge Cases
- What happens when the model is wrong?
- How do you handle low-confidence outputs?
- Where do you escalate to humans?
- Have you tested adversarial or jailbreak scenarios?
- How do you prevent harmful, repetitive, or generic outputs?
- How do you ensure consistency over time?

### 9. Technical Approach
- Why did you choose this architecture?
- Why use RAG / agents / structured outputs here?
- What inputs does the system rely on?
- What part was technically hardest?
- What tradeoffs did you make?
- What would you change if rebuilding from scratch?

### 10. Metrics / Business Impact
- What metric proves this is working?
- Are you measuring outputs or outcomes?
- What customer KPI improves?
- How do you know users are getting value?
- What metric would convince a buyer to renew?
- What does ROI look like?

### 11. GTM / Commercialization
- Who do you sell to first?
- How do you reach them?
- What is the adoption motion?
- What objections do you expect in the sales process?
- How do you get initial pilots?
- What market are you entering first?

### 12. Pricing / Monetization
- How do you charge?
- Why this pricing model?
- What does usage cost you?
- How do costs scale with adoption?
- Is pricing aligned with delivered value?
- How do you think about free vs paid users?

### 13. Expansion / Roadmap
- What is the next feature or step?
- What is the more ambitious version of this?
- How does this expand over time?
- What adjacent workflows could you own next?
- What would you build after proving the wedge?
- What does version 2 look like?

### 14. Presentation / Demo Quality
- Did the demo clearly show the differentiated value?
- Was the user journey easy to follow?
- Did you show the actual moment of value?
- Did the demo feel too generic?
- Was the product story clear and credible?
- Did the presentation match the claimed impact?
