## Pattern of questions

The judges repeatedly focused on:

- **User workflow / UX friction**
- **What AI adds beyond a generic LLM**
- **Target customer clarity**
- **Go-to-market and customer acquisition**
- **Validation of the problem**
- **Technical feasibility and implementation details**
- **Accuracy / reliability / safety**
- **Differentiation from incumbents or existing alternatives**
- **Scalability to a real product**

---

## Main areas of focus in the questions

### 1. Problem clarity and validation
- Is this a real pain point?
- Who exactly has the problem?
- Was the problem validated with users or research?

### 2. Product design and usability
- Is the workflow too manual?
- Can onboarding be simpler?
- Is the experience intuitive and practical?

### 3. AI value-add
- Why does this need AI?
- What makes this better than using ChatGPT or another LLM directly?
- Does AI materially improve speed, personalization, or relevance?

### 4. Go-to-market
- Who is the ICP?
- How will users find the product?
- Can acquisition economics work?

### 5. Technical robustness
- How does it work under the hood?
- Is the system accurate enough?
- Can it scale beyond a demo?

### 6. Risk, trust, and compliance
- Privacy concerns
- Safety concerns
- Liability and compliance, especially in healthcare and surveillance

### 7. Strategic defensibility
- Why this instead of incumbents?
- Why wouldn’t existing platforms build it themselves?
- What is the durable differentiation?

## Capstone judge question framework / checklist

### 1. Problem
- What exact problem are you solving?
- Who feels this pain most acutely?
- How often does it happen?
- How painful / expensive is it?

### 2. Customer
- Who is the ICP?
- Who is the actual user?
- Who is the buyer?
- What segment will you start with first?

### 3. Validation
- Did you speak to users?
- What evidence shows this is a real problem?
- What behaviors or workflows confirm the need?
- Why is now the right time?

### 4. Why AI
- Why does this need AI at all?
- Why is AI better than rules, search, or manual workflow?
- What does AI enable that wasn’t practical before?
- Why is this better than a general LLM prompt?

### 5. Product experience
- Is the workflow intuitive?
- Where is there too much user effort?
- Can onboarding be simplified?
- What is the fastest path to value?

### 6. Demo quality
- Did the demo clearly show the problem, product, and outcome?
- Did it show user value quickly?
- Was it easy to follow?
- Did the output feel actionable, not just interesting?

### 7. Technical design
- How does the system work end to end?
- What models, agents, retrieval, or tools are used?
- Where does the data come from?
- How do you evaluate output quality?

### 8. Accuracy / safety / trust
- How accurate does it need to be?
- What happens when it’s wrong?
- How do you handle unsafe outputs?
- What guardrails, feedback loops, or human review exist?

### 9. Differentiation
- Why wouldn’t ChatGPT alone do this?
- Why wouldn’t an incumbent build this?
- What is unique or defensible here?
- Why will users choose this over alternatives?

### 10. Go-to-market
- How will you get users?
- What is the first wedge?
- What channel will work?
- Are acquisition costs realistic?

### 11. Business model
- Who pays?
- For what?
- Why will they pay?
- Does the monetization fit the user and workflow?

### 12. Scalability / roadmap
- What would make this production-ready?
- What are the biggest blockers?
- What would you build next?
- What assumptions still need testing?

## Fast prep checklist before presenting

- Clear one-line problem statement
- Clear ICP and first beachhead market
- Evidence the problem is real
- Strong answer for “why AI?”
- Strong answer for “why not just ChatGPT?”
- Demo shows value in under 60 seconds
- Realistic explanation of workflow
- Explanation of data sources
- Evaluation method for quality/safety
- Thoughtful GTM plan
- Clear differentiation from incumbents
- Honest next steps / risks

---

## Best way to answer judge questions

Use this structure:

**Problem → User → Why AI → Demo value → Feasibility → Risks → Next steps**

Example:
- Problem: who has it and why it matters
- User: exactly who you’re serving first
- Why AI: what AI uniquely unlocks
- Demo value: what the user gets immediately
- Feasibility: how it works and where data comes from
- Risks: what could go wrong
- Next steps: how you’d validate or productionize it
